Ropa Vieja

Description

Ropa vieja means “old clothes” in Spanish. It’s probably named this way because the meat is shredded so it ends up looking like rags. Serve it with a side of white rice and some plantains (tostones or maduros) and it makes am excellent and filling dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 2 1/2 pounds flank steak (or any other marbled cut of beef like chuck), cut in 3-inch x 4-inch pieces
  • Goya Adobo with Pepper, to taste
  • 2 large yellow onions, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 green bell peppers, finely chopped
  • 1 (6 ounce) jar Goya Sofrito
  • 3 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 packet Sazon Goya with Coriander and Annatto
  • 1 packet beef bouillon
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup Spanish olives stuffed with minced pimientos, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro (optional)
  • 2 cups cooked long grain rice

Directions

  1. Heat oil in large heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Season meat with adobo.
  3. Add steak to pot and cook in batches, flipping once, until well browned, about 10 minutes.
  4. Transfer meat to a plate.
  5. Lower heat to medium.
  6. Stir in onions and peppers. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are soft, about 10 minutes.
  7. Add Sofrito and garlic to pot; cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  8. Stir in 3 of cups water, tomato sauce, Sazón, beef bouillon and pepper.
  9. Bring mixture to a boil.
  10. Return beef to pot.
  11. Lower heat to medium low and simmer, covered, stirring occasionally, about 45 minutes.
  12. Stir in olives.
  13. Cover pot; simmer until meat shreds easily with fork, 1 – 1½ hours, adding more water in ¼ cup measurements, if pot becomes dry.
  14. Transfer meat to work surface; shred meat with two forks.
  15. Bring remaining sauce to a boil.
  16. Mix meat and cilantro (optional) into sauce.
  17. Serve with white rice.
